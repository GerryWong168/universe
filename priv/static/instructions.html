★ UNIVERSE ★

A Classic Star Trek Space Combat Game

📜 Historical Background

The Universe game (also known as Star Trek or Super Star Trek) has its origins in the golden age of mainframe computing during the late 1960s and early 1970s.

The Original Game (1971-1974)

The first Star Trek game was created in 1971 by Mike Mayfield and written in BASIC for the Sigma 7 mainframe computer at the University of California, Irvine. The game was inspired by the original Star Trek television series (1966-1969) and the strategic board game "Battleship."

  • 1971: Mike Mayfield creates the original Star Trek game in BASIC
  • 1972-1973: The game spreads across university mainframes via DECUS (Digital Equipment Computer Users' Society)
  • 1974: David H. Ahl publishes "Super Star Trek" in his book "101 BASIC Computer Games"
  • 1975-1980s: Countless variations appear on PDP-11, VAX, Honeywell Multics, and other systems

The Mainframe Era

During the 1970s, this game was played on timesharing systems at universities and research institutions worldwide:

  • PDP-11 and VAX systems running RSTS/E, RSX-11, and VMS
  • Honeywell Multics at institutions like MIT and the University of Calgary
  • IBM mainframes running TSO and VM/CMS
  • HP 2000 and 3000 timesharing systems
  • IP Sharp APL systems with APL-based variants

Cultural Impact

Star Trek/Universe became one of the most popular computer games of the 1970s, introducing an entire generation to computer gaming. It demonstrated that computers could provide entertainment beyond calculations, and its text-based interface made it accessible on terminals with limited graphics capabilities.

💡 This modern graphical version recreates the classic gameplay with a Millennium Falcon-inspired cockpit interface, bringing the retro mainframe experience to the web!

🎯 Mission Objective

You are the captain of the USS ENTERPRISE, the Federation's most advanced starship. Your mission is to eliminate all Klingon warships threatening Federation space before your stardates run out.

⚠️ WARNING: The Klingons will fire back! Manage your shields and energy carefully.

🎮 Game Controls

Navigation

  • ↑ UP - Move ship north (decrease sector row)
  • ↓ DOWN - Move ship south (increase sector row)
  • ← LEFT - Move ship west (decrease sector column)
  • → RIGHT - Move ship east (increase sector column)

💡 Tip: Moving to the edge of a sector will automatically warp you to the adjacent quadrant.

Weapons Systems

  • FIRE PHASERS - Energy weapon with adjustable power (50-500 units)
    • Use the power slider to set phaser strength
    • Damage decreases with distance
    • Consumes energy from ship's reserves
  • PHOTON TORPEDOES - Powerful projectile weapons
    • Click directional buttons (N, S, E, W) to fire
    • Limited supply (starts with 10)
    • Travels in straight line until hitting target or edge
    • Instant destruction on direct hit

Sensors & Scanners

  • SHORT RANGE SCAN - Shows all entities in current quadrant
    • Reveals Klingons, starbases, and stars
    • Updates tactical display
    • Low energy cost
  • LONG RANGE SCAN - Shows adjacent quadrants
    • Displays Klingon count in surrounding 8 quadrants
    • Helps plan strategic movement
    • Moderate energy cost

Ship Systems

  • SHIELDS - Protective energy barrier
    • Enter amount to allocate from ship's energy
    • Absorbs enemy fire
    • Shields regenerate slowly when not under attack
  • DOCK AT STARBASE - Repair and resupply
    • Must be adjacent to a starbase (blue "B")
    • Fully restores energy, shields, and torpedoes
    • Repairs all damage
    • Use strategically when low on resources

🗺️ Understanding the Display

Tactical Grid (10×10 Sector View)

Symbol Meaning Description
◆ (Green) Your Ship USS ENTERPRISE - your position in the sector
K (Red) Klingon Warship Enemy vessel - destroy on sight!
B (Blue) Starbase Federation outpost - dock here to repair
* (Yellow) Star Obstacle - cannot move through
· (Gray) Empty Space Safe to navigate

HUD Information

  • Quadrant: Your current position in the 8×8 galaxy grid
  • Sector: Your position within the current quadrant (0-9, 0-9)
  • Energy: Ship's power reserves (used for movement, weapons, shields)
  • Shields: Defensive barrier strength
  • Torpedoes: Remaining photon torpedo count
  • Klingons: Total enemy ships remaining in galaxy
  • Stardates: Time remaining to complete mission
  • Score: Points earned (100 per Klingon destroyed)

💡 Strategy & Tips

Combat Tactics

  1. Maintain Distance: Phaser damage decreases with range. Get close for maximum effect.
  2. Use Torpedoes Wisely: Save torpedoes for distant targets or when energy is low.
  3. Shield Management: Raise shields before entering quadrants with Klingons.
  4. Energy Conservation: Every action costs energy. Plan your moves carefully.

Exploration Strategy

  1. Use Long Range Scan: Scout ahead to avoid entering heavily defended quadrants.
  2. Find Starbases Early: Locate repair facilities before engaging in heavy combat.
  3. Clear Systematically: Work through the galaxy methodically rather than randomly.
  4. Watch Your Stardates: Don't waste time - each move consumes time.

Resource Management

  • Energy Priority: Shields > Weapons > Movement
  • Dock Frequently: Don't wait until critically damaged
  • Phaser Efficiency: Lower power settings work fine at close range
  • Torpedo Conservation: You only get 10 - make them count!

🏆 Winning & Losing

Victory Conditions

Destroy all Klingon warships in the galaxy before your stardates expire. Your final score is based on:

  • Klingons destroyed (100 points each)
  • Remaining stardates (bonus for efficiency)
  • Remaining energy and torpedoes

Defeat Conditions

  • Ship Destroyed: Energy reaches zero while under attack
  • Time Expired: Stardates run out before mission complete
  • Stranded: Insufficient energy to reach a starbase

🎓 Historical Gameplay Notes

This modern version preserves the core mechanics of the 1970s original while adding visual enhancements:

  • Real-time Combat: Klingons fire back immediately (original was turn-based)
  • Graphical Display: Visual grid replaces ASCII text output
  • Mouse Controls: Buttons replace typed commands
  • Animated Effects: Starfield and glowing HUD elements

💡 In the original 1971 version, players typed commands like "NAV", "PHA", "TOR", and "SRS" at a teletype terminal. This version brings that classic experience to the modern web!
